About the Site


"Data analysis" is a terribly broad term. Academically, it draws from the fields of statistics and computer science, but calling it an academic topic would be as odd as calling "researching a paper" an academic topic. Rather, this site presents data analysis as the practical toolkit for quantitatively formulating, analyzing, and defending your research in a professional setting.

Goals for this site:

  • Distill the wide range of data-analysis topics into a curriculum relevant to IPS, based on my experience
  • Present the best and established open-source tools, and the user communities around them
  • Create a question bank and technical infrastrucure for practicing data skills on policy-related datasets
  • Supplement quantitative courses in the IPS curriculum that teach mathematics
  • And a lofty one: foster within IPS a community of quantitative policy -makers and -influencers, and provide a forum to develop that community

What this site is not:

  • A replacement for any Statistics curriculum
  • A place to learn the absolute basics of R, SQL, or Excel. There are many excellent tutorials out there (see Other Resources). The content assumes the reader has already learned the syntax and some fundamentals.

About Me


I've been working for a data science and analytics team at a financial company, SoFi, since 2014. The company has grown significantly over the last few years, and as such I've had the opportunity to test and develop my own skillset as well as the those of colleagues and job candidates. While my recent career experience is outside the policy field, the core elements of data analysis will be common to many of your careers, and my knowledge in this field is what I hope to share.

I created this site to give back to the IPS community. I hope you find it useful. If you have any questions, comments, tips, or the desire to help out with content then please email me or submit them on the comments form. My engagement level, and the breadth and depth of content, is in direct proportion to the engagement that the IPS community is interested in. I hope to hear from you!
